What Is Vibe Coding?

The term was coined by Andrej Karpathy — co-founder of OpenAI and former Director of AI at Tesla — in a single post on X on February 3, 2025. It spread through the developer world in hours and within weeks had become the defining phrase for an entirely new way of building software.

"There's a new kind of coding I call 'vibe coding', where you fully give in to the vibes, embrace exponentials, and forget that the code even exists."

— Andrej Karpathy, OpenAI Co-Founder, February 2025

The idea is deceptively simple: instead of writing code manually, you describe what you want in plain language to an AI. The AI writes the code. You review, test, iterate, and ship. No syntax memorization. No Stack Overflow rabbit holes at 2am. No hours debugging a misplaced semicolon.

But vibe coding is not the same as no-code. It's not drag-and-drop. You're still building real software — with real, functional code under the hood. The creative direction, the product logic, the UX decisions — those are entirely yours. The AI is your technical co-pilot, not your creative director.

Key Distinction

No-code tools limit you to what their platform can do. Vibe coding has no ceiling — if you can describe it clearly enough, AI can build it. The constraint is your imagination and your ability to direct it.

The Vibe Coding Workflow — Step by Step

Here's the actual workflow. Not the theoretical version — what I actually do when starting a new project with AI.

The vibe coding loop: describe → generate → review → iterate
01

Define the idea with precision

This is where creative people have a structural advantage. The clearer your brief, the better the output. Think like an art director briefing a developer: what does it do, who uses it, what should it look, feel, and behave like? AI rewards specificity. Vague prompts produce vague software.

02

Choose the right AI stack for the job

For most projects: Cursor + Claude 3.7 Sonnet. For UI-heavy front-end: v0 by Vercel to generate the React components, then Cursor to add logic. For full applications fast: Bolt.new or Lovable. The tool choice matters — different AI tools have different strengths.

03

Write a strong scaffolding prompt

Your first prompt should describe the full project — tech stack preference, structure, core features, design language, user flow. Think of it as writing a proper creative brief for a senior developer. "Build a SaaS dashboard for brand managers that tracks campaign performance..." is far more powerful than "make a website."

04

Iterate conversationally

This is the heartbeat of vibe coding. You talk to the AI like a developer sitting next to you. "Move the button to the header." "Make the sidebar collapsible." "Add error states to the form." Each iteration is a creative conversation, not a code review. You direct; it executes.

05

Review for behavior, not syntax

You don't need to understand every line of generated code. You need to understand what the app does and whether it does it correctly. Test every user flow. Break it deliberately — give it bad inputs, edge cases, and unexpected sequences. The AI will fix what you find.

06

Ship and iterate in the real world

Vercel, Netlify, Railway, Render — most vibe-coded apps go live in under 10 minutes. Shipping early gives you real user feedback, which makes the next round of AI-directed changes far more focused and valuable than anything you could anticipate in isolation.

Why Creatives Have the Edge

Here's the thing nobody talks about: vibe coding inherently favors people with creative judgment over people with deep technical knowledge.

A senior developer with a decade of experience might actually be slower to ship with AI than a creative director who's never written a single function — because the developer's trained instinct is to control the technical details, while the creative director's instinct is to define the experience and judge the output.

"Vibe coding is 80% direction and 20% execution. It rewards exactly the skills that come from years of creative work."

— Arya Sen

The skills that make vibe coding powerful are:

The Creative Advantage in Vibe Coding

Clear visual judgment — knowing immediately when something looks wrong or right.

Strong product instinct — understanding what the user actually needs, not just what they asked for.

Brief-writing ability — being able to describe what you want precisely enough that someone else (or an AI) can build it exactly.

Iteration discipline — knowing when to keep refining and when to stop. Not every pass needs to be perfect.

Taste under pressure — making fast creative decisions when the AI generates multiple options.

After 16 years of retouching — where my entire job was making something look exactly right through hundreds of micro-decisions — vibe coding feels natural to me. The AI generates a rough pass. I direct it toward better. I know when it's wrong. I know when it's right. The craft is in the direction, not the execution.

Common Vibe Coding Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

Mistake 1: Vague, directionless prompts

"Make it look better" is not a brief. "Increase the card padding to 32px, shift to a lighter border color, and make the heading size 18px bold" is a brief. The more specific and visual your direction, the better the output. Treat prompting like writing a design spec.

Mistake 2: Not testing deliberately

AI-generated code can pass a visual check and still break under edge cases. Test every user flow. Enter bad inputs. Try it on different screen sizes. Break it on purpose. The AI will fix what you find — but you have to find it first.

Mistake 3: Over-engineering version one

The single biggest mistake in any startup or product project: building too much before validating. Ship the simplest version that proves the concept. Add features after real users tell you what they actually want.

Mistake 4: Ignoring security entirely

AI doesn't think about security by default. For anything handling user data, payments, or authentication, always run a dedicated prompt: "Review this entire codebase for security vulnerabilities and fix them." Do it before every deployment.

Mistake 5: Trying to fix a broken context window

When an AI session gets stuck in a loop — generating the same bad output repeatedly — stop. Don't try to fix a broken session by feeding it more bad context. Start a fresh conversation with a clean, well-structured summary of the problem. Fresh context almost always produces better results.

The Future of Vibe Coding

Vibe coding is not a trend that will pass when the next framework arrives. It's a structural shift in how software gets made — as significant as the shift from Photoshop desktop to browser-based design tools, or from film to digital photography.

Within three to five years, we will see:

What's Coming

AI agents that test and deploy autonomously — writing, testing, and shipping code without human review for defined, low-risk tasks.

Full product studios run by one person — a single creative founder with AI agents handling development, QA, marketing copy, and customer support.

The designer/developer boundary disappearing — the most valuable creative people will be those who can direct both visual craft and product logic with equal fluency.

New creative roles that don't exist yet — AI Art Director, AI Product Architect, Creative Systems Designer. The job titles of 2030 don't have names yet.

The question isn't whether to adopt vibe coding. For creatives who rely on digital products, tools, and presence — it's already a competitive moat. The question is how fast you can develop the taste, judgment, and brief-writing clarity that makes it genuinely powerful.

The Bottom Line

Vibe coding doesn't replace developers. It removes the gap between creative people and their ideas.

If you have years of visual craft behind you — retouching, design, photography, art direction, advertising — you already have 80% of what makes vibe coding powerful. You know what good looks like. You know when something is wrong. You know what you're trying to build and why.

The AI handles the syntax. You handle the vision. That's not a shortcut. That's the creative superpower.
